    Julia Stiles bio

    Julia Stiles is an American actress. Born and raised in New York City, Stiles began acting at the age of 11 as part of New York’s La MaMa Experimental Theatre Club.

    Stiles was born in New York City to Judith Newcomb Stiles, an artist from Greenwich Village, and John O’Hara, a businessman. She is the eldest of three siblings, with her brother John Junior and sister Jane, who is also an actress. Stiles has a mixed heritage, with English, Irish, and Italian roots. At the age of 11, she began her acting career with the renowned La MaMa Theatre Company in New York.

    Her first appearance on the big screen was a minor role in the 1996 film “I Love You, I Love You Not” in 1996. However, it was her leading role in “Wicked” in 1998 that earned her the prestigious Karlovy Vary Film Festival Award for Best Actress. Stiles gained widespread recognition for her performances in popular teen films like “10 Things I Hate About You” (1999), “Down to You” (2000), and “Save the Last Dance” (2001). Throughout her career, she has received numerous accolades, including a Teen Choice Award, two MTV Movie Awards, and nominations for a Golden Globe Award and a Primetime Emmy Award.

    In addition to her notable roles in films such as “The Business of Strangers” (2001), “Mona Lisa Smile” (2003), and “The Omen” (2006), Stiles became a global sensation with her portrayal of Nicky Parsons in the Bourne franchise from 2002 to 2016. She has also appeared in other noteworthy films like “Hamlet,” “State and Main” (both 2000), “O” (2001), “A Guy Thing” (2002), “Carolina” (2003), “The Prince & Me” (2004), “Edmond,” “A Little Trip to Heaven” (both 2005), “The Cry of the Owl” (2009), “Silver Linings Playbook” (2012), “Out of the Dark” (2014), “Blackway” (2015), “11:55” (2016), “Hustlers” (2019), and “Orphan: First Kill” (2022).

    Julia Stiles: Why is she trending?

    During a skit on the show Saturday, the talented actress delighted fans with her reprised role in the 2001 film. In a surprise cameo on Saturday Night Live, the 42-year-old actress showcased her dance skills and brought back the beloved character Sara from “Save the Last Dance.”

    The hilarious performance skit was introduced by SNL’s Chloe Fineman, who was dressed in a Christmas robe and presented a “sexy gift idea” for the holidays during Weekend Update, hosted by Colin Jost and Michael Che.

    “The perfect holiday gift this year is the dance that Julia Stiles does at the end of the 2001 movie Save the Last Dance,” Fineman, 35, said. She then disrobed to reveal an all-black outfit matching the final performance look of Stiles’ character from the film. 

    Fineman then went into a full dance routine which she coined, “Street/Ballet”, resembling Sara’s last dance. 

    “So does she get into Juliard…?” Jost, 41, asked Fineman. 

    Stiles then jumped into the frame as she responded, “Yes!” while wearing an outfit that matched Fineman’s and her own slick bun. “Sara did get into Julliard,” she added, as the audience cheered. 

    “How?” Jost asked her. “Well, you didn’t see the end of the dance,” Stiles replied as Fineman shouted, “Hit it!” The pair then performed a synchronized final part of their Save the Last Dance routine as they sat on chairs, finishing with a fist bump.

    “OK, so Colin, what did you think?” Fineman asked Jost. 

    “Well I can’t say this on the record yet…but welcome to Juliard!” Jost said.

    Who are Julia Stiles children?

    Julia Stiles and her husband, Preston J. Cook, are proud parents of two children named Strummer Newcomb and Arlo. In 2022, upon the arrival of their second child, the talented actress took to Instagram to share the joyous news.

    “Welcome to the world, Baby Arlo! ✨ The newest addition to our family, reminding me how infinite love can be. 💖,” Stiles writes alongside a photo of baby Arlo’s feet.
