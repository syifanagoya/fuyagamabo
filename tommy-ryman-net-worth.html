    Tommy Ryman Full Overview and Wiki

    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Tommy Ryman, born on March 17, 1983, embarked on his journey in the Minnesota comedy scene, drawing early inspiration from the late Mitch Hedberg.

    His mother, Barb Ryman, a folk singer-songwriter, undoubtedly influenced his creative path. Ryman’s clean humor and observational comedy quickly set him apart in the industry.

    Career Highlights

    Ryman’s career took a significant turn when he became a semi finalist on Season 8 of Last Comic Standing. This exposure catapulted him into the limelight, leading to his recognition by Paste Magazine as one of the 12 Great Stand-Up Comedians from the Midwest.

    His style, characterized by clean humor, observational comedy, sarcasm, and satire, resonated with audiences, earning him the title of Best Local Standup Comedian by City Pages in 2016.

    Comedy Style and Recognition

    Known for refraining from off-color humor, Ryman’s style is a blend of sarcasm, satire, and observational comedy.

    This unique approach earned him a spot in VidAngel’s Dry Bar Comedy Season 3 series, titled Activated.

    His performances at notable events like the Great American Comedy Festival at the Johnny Carson Theater and Gilda’s Laugh Fest, where he won the Best of Midwest award, further showcase his comedic prowess.
