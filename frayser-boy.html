    Frayser Boy Facts

  • On August 26, 2003, Frayser Boy released his debut studio album titled Gone on That Bay through Hypnotize Minds and it included songs such as Nan Notha’ (ft. DJ Paul and Juicy J), I’ll Bring Da Weed, Bloody Murder, Bay Area (ft. Lil Wyte), Watchin’ Me (ft. DJ Paul), Gone On That Bay, H.C.P. (ft. DJ Paul, Crunchy Black, Juicy J, Lord Infamous, and Lil Wyte), Closed Mouth, Niggaz Wild Throwin’ Bows, I Had To Get’m, Wish A Mutha Would, Hydro Weed, and Every Day Thang.
  • The album Gone on That Bay was ranked at #6 on the US Heatseekers Albums (Billboard) chart, at #9 on the US Independent Albums (Billboard) chart, and at #178 on the US Billboard 200 chart.
  • Frayser Boy’s 2nd studio album Me Being Me was released on July 12, 2005, and it consists of tracks like I Got Dat Drank (ft. Paul Wall and Mike Jones), Ain’t Nothin’ Changed, Stay Focused, She Got Me Sayin Damn (ft. Chrome and Juicy J), and Posse Song (ft. Lil Wyte, Lord Infamous, Juicy J, DJ Paul, Boogie Mane, Crunchy Black, and Chrome). The album peaked at #3 on the US Heatseekers Albums (Billboard) chart, at #15 on the US Top Rap Albums (Billboard) chart, and at #24 on the US Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums (Billboard) chart.
  • He released his 3rd studio album Da Key on May 20, 2008, and it was ranked at #8 on the US Top Rap Albums (Billboard) chart, at #13 on the US Heatseekers Albums (Billboard) chart, and at #22 on the US Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums (Billboard) chart.
  • Frayser Boy appeared as Terrence in the drama film Dig That, Zeebo Newton in 2018.
