Several renowned films, such as the interesting Tár, the frightening Armageddon Time, and the thrilling M3GAN, will be available on Prime Video in June in addition to these engaging series.

    Below are the best Top Ten TV shows and films that premiere in June 2023. Your current Amazon subscription includes all these shows at no additional cost.

    10. An Unforgettable Year – Summer (2023)

    Becoming a fashion designer in Paris is a lifelong goal for Inha, a young village girl. To make this ambition a reality, you’ll need a curriculum that blows the judges away. She realizes she needs to act quickly if she wants to land a job in the fashion industry, so she takes advantage of a trip to Portela to try out for a position as a seamstress. Carrie Catherine, a costume designer with an international reputation, is one of her coworkers, much to her surprise.

    9. That Peter Crouch Film (2023)

    That Peter Crouch Film, a new documentary about one of modern football‘s most distinctive and popular figures, will soon be available on Prime Video. The film will provide viewers with intimate access to Crouch, from his first encounter with the booing public during his professional debut to his marriage to Abbey. On June 22, 2023, the world will see the UK Original documentary film, which will only be available on Prime Video. 

    8. Deadlock TV Series (2023)

    The beautiful Tasmanian hamlet of Deadlock is preparing for its annual Winter Festival when tragedy strikes and a local citizen is discovered dead. After this tragic event, two experienced detectives and an enthusiastic young constable are sent to investigate the crime. They are desperate to learn the truth about what happened.

    7. Medellin (2023)

    The film is a comedy about a man named Reda who organizes a team to raid the kidnappers in Colombia so that he can rescue his brother from the narcos in Medellin. Medellin is premiering on Prime Video on June 02, an exciting new action film starring French actor Franck Gastambide. Reda (Gastambide) sends a group of people to Colombia to rescue his kidnapped brother.

    6. Shiny Happy People: Duggar Family Secrets (2023)

    The premise of the fascinating limited documentary series Shiny Happy People: Duggar Family Secrets is simple: reveal the truth behind reality TV’s most beloved mega family, the Duggars. The series’ introspective investigation reveals the shocking realities beneath the surface. It investigates the group’s foundational institution, the Institute of Basic Life Principles (IBLP), which has generated widespread controversy.

    5. With Love Season 2 (2023) 

    The second season of With Love picks off just where the first left off, with an exciting resolution to the Season 1 cliffhanger. Sol and Miles, Lily and Santiago, and Jorge and Henry are the focal points of this exciting chapter. The long-awaited answer to who popped the question ushers in a season of joyous wedding planning and mounting excitement in the run-up to the big day. Intricate details and touching moments from the couples’ paths to the altar will keep viewers riveted.

    4. My Fault (2023)

    My Fault is the story of Noah. When Noah’s mother Rafaela marries the wealthy William Leister, he is uprooted from his hometown, lover, and friends. However, at 17, Noah is strong-willed and refuses to give up his independence for the sake of a life of opulence. There, she meets Nick, her new stepbrother, and the tension between their dominant egos is immediately palpable.

    3. The Lake Season 2 (2023)

    The second season of “The Lake,” a captivating Amazon Original Series from Canada, is now available to watch. As Justin and Riley navigate the complexities of their relationship, you can expect your emotions to go up and down with them. At the same time, Maisy-May is trapped in a flurry of contradictory feelings as she tries to process her mother’s unexpected return.

    2. I’m A Virgo (2023)

    The coming-of-age comedy I’m A Virgo stars (Jharrel Jerome, who won an Emmy in 2019 for his lead performance in the mini-series “When They See Us.” “I’m a Virgo” follows a 13-foot-tall man named Cootie (Jerome), a native of Oakland, Calif., who spends his time binging TV shows and comic series. When he encounters his real-life superhero, dubbed The Hero (Goggins), Cootie finds himself on course for a mythical quest.

    1. Tom Clancy’s Jack Ryan Season 4 (2023)

    Author Tom Clancy introduced readers to Jack Ryan in a book series, and several films followed. For the first time, the former U.S. Marine is the focus of an episodic television series, portrayed by John Krasinski in an Amazon original thriller centered on Ryan’s career as a rising CIA analyst. As his first field job, he is thrown into peril and discovers a pattern in terrorists’ messaging.
