    FAQs about Anne Bancroft

    What was Anne Bancroft’s birth name?

    She was born as Anna Maria Louisa Italiano in the Bronx, New York. She changed her name to Anne Bancroft early in her acting career.

    Was Anne Bancroft married?

    Yes, she was married to comedian and filmmaker Mel Brooks for over 40 years until her death. They tied the knot in 1964 and had one son together.

    What are some of Bancroft’s most notable works?

    Her most notable works include The Graduate (1967), The Miracle Worker (1962), The Elephant Man (1980), and Blazing Saddles (1974).

    What did Bancroft achieve on Broadway?

    She had a successful Broadway career, winning a Tony Award for The Miracle Worker. She was also the first woman to win a Tony for Best Direction of a Play.

    What was her connection to Mel Brooks’ movies?

    She appeared in the 1983 film To Be or Not to Be, a remake of the 1942 film, produced by Mel Brooks.

    How many Academy Award nominations did Bancroft receive?

    She received five Academy Award nominations for her roles in The Pumpkin Eater, The Graduate, The Turning Point, The Elephant Man, and Agnes of God.

    What awards did she win for her role in The Miracle Worker?

    For her performance as Annie Sullivan, she won the Academy Award for Best Actress, a BAFTA Award, and the National Society of Film Critics Award.

    When did Anne Bancroft pass away, and what was the cause of her death?

    She died on June 6, 2005, due to uterine cancer.

    How much did Anne Bancroft earn per movie?

    For some of her early roles like Tonight We Sing and Treasure of the Golden Condor, she reportedly earned around $500 per week. Later, her salary increased with successful projects like The Slender Thread and The Graduate
