    Introduction 

    Example is an English musician, singer, songwriter, rapper and record producer from London.

    His name arose due to his initials being E.G., which is an abbreviation of the Latin phrase exempli gratia (“for example”).

    As of January 2024, Example’s net worth is estimated to be $12 Million.

    Early Life

    Elliot John Gleave was born on the 20th of June, 1982, in London.

    Gleave is the son of Elaine Johnston and Michael Gleave.

    In a 2012 interview with The Guardian, he said “I was diagnosed with Asperger’s when I was younger. I was amazing with numbers, I had a photographic memory, and I hated reading fiction, preferring books on nature and history. All symptoms of mild autism.”

    Career 

    Example’s career kicked off after he released three singles in 2006, earning him attention from Pete Tong and Zane Lowe on Radio 1. 

    Soon after, he signed a deal with The Beats and released the single “Vile” in response to Lily Allen’s number one hit, “Smile.” 

    His first official release through The Beats was “What We Made,” and the music video for it was filmed in Chornobyl, Ukraine. 

    Example’s second Beats single was “You Can’t Rap” in 2007, followed by a re-release of his self-released single “I Don’t Want To.” 

    After the Beats label went under, Example started self-releasing his work again and even tried stand-up comedy in 2008. 

    Finding Commercial Success

    Example had his first commercial success in September 2009 with “Watch the Sun Come Up,” the lead single on his second album, Won’t Go Quitely. 

    This single debuted at no. 20 on the UK Singles Chart and was followed by the single “Won’t Go Quitely,” which debuted at no. 6. 

    When his second album dropped, it debuted at no. 4 on the UK Albums Chart, thanks to the prior popularity of his singles. 

    In May 2010, Example collaborated with Ed Sheeran on a song about Nandos, one of the most popular restaurants in the UK. 

    A year later, Example released the first single from his third album, “Changed the Way You Kiss Me,” which debuted at no. 1 on the UK charts. 

    His third album, Playing in the Shadows, dropped in September 2011 at no. 1 in the UK and no. 6 in Ireland. 

    Hit Singles And Albums

    In 2012, Example wrote “Chasing The Sun” for The Wanted, which was later remixed by Hardwell and hit no. 2 on the UK Singles Chart. 

    The same year, Example signed a record deal in the US with Mercury, a branch of Universal, with the goal of growing his American audience. 

    2012 was also the year he released his fourth studio album, The Evolution of Man, which peaked at no. 13 on the UK charts. 

    He followed this up by debuting the lead single from his fifth album in 2013, titled “All the Wrong Places,” at a concert in Auckland. 

    In 2013, he signed with the UK division of Epic Records and appeared at the Glastonbury Festival, where he played tracks from his upcoming album. 

    Like many of the richest singers in the world, Example became involved in charity work, serving as an ambassador for the Teenage Cancer Trust. 

    His fifth album, Live Life Living, was dropped in 2014, which once again became a hit on the charts in the UK and Ireland. 

    Collaborations And Success

    Before his 2014 tour, Example revealed he was collaborating with Netsky and Sigma on his upcoming sixth album. 

    While he took a brief break in 2015 to spend time with his family, the following year, he came back and announced the single “Later.” 

    In 2018, he released five singles before independently releasing his mixtape, Bangers & Ballads, which has since been deemed to be his sixth album. 

    His 2019 single “All Night” hit no. 26 on the UK Dance Chart, marking his first chart appearance since 2015. 

    Shortly after, he released the single “Back on the Wreck,” his first self-produced single that he didn’t provide vocals for. 

    That year also saw him release his seventh album, Some Nights Last For Days, which he quickly turned out during quarantine. 

    Hard At Work

    Example signed with BMG in 2021 and quickly dropped “Every Single Time,” the first single from his eighth upcoming album. 

    Throughout 2022, he consistently released new singles from the album, many of which featured collaborations with other artists. 

    While he wasn’t making tracks with the richest rappers in the world, he did collab with Lucy Lucy, Kanine, and Penny Ivy. 

    His eighth album, We May Grow Old But We Never Grow Up, was released in June and hit no. 5 on the UK Dance Albums Chart. 

    Most recently, he appeared at the Glastonbury Festival in 2023, with headliners including Elton John and Guns N’ Roses.
