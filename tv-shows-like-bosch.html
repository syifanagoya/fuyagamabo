    Drama about a good cop TV shows are excellent time fillers, and once you’ve been addicted to them, it’s difficult to avoid binge-watching them. ‘Bosch,’ for example, is a fantastic cop drama series that is based on Michael Connelly’s acclaimed books and does a fantastic job of honoring the source material. We’re not always sure if TV shows can elicit the same emotions as the books they’re based on, but what ‘Bosch’ manages to depict is completely strange and unfolds precisely as you envisioned while reading the book. Even if you aren’t much of a reader and haven’t read the original books, this series will keep you awake at night with its nerve-wracking suspense and puzzles. This jazz-themed performance is one of the best in the genre, and the only problem is that, like all good things in life, it must come to an end at some point.

    Like most great badass cops, Harry Bosch (Titus Welliver), the protagonist of this drama, has significant authority issues and throws himself into serious difficulties when he murders a cold-blooded serial killer. But following his instincts and breaking a few laws along the way has helped him to grow into the man he is today, with the top case-solving rate in the state of California. While Bosch is awaiting a federal trial for killing a serial killer, he comes across the case of a 13-year-old boy’s murder, which may be the most difficult case he has ever had to solve.

    Titus Welliver, who plays the major role with a gloomy tone, does an excellent job of portraying the man depicted in the books. This isn’t your run-of-the-mill cop drama, and you’ll be astonished at how excellent it is. If you’ve finished watching ‘Bosch,’ you’re undoubtedly suffering from the after-binge-viewing blues. That’s the real deal now. As a result, we’ve compiled a list of the finest shows that are comparable to ‘Bosch.’ Several of these shows, such as ‘Bosch,’ are available on Netflix, Hulu, or Amazon Prime.

    Broadchurch (2013)


    When the murder of an 11-year-old kid shatters the quiet and welcoming coastal village of Broadchurch, everything appears to be perfectly fine. This draws unwanted media attention to the village, and soon the deepest truths that this location hides begin to surface. Locals in the town begin to open up about everything they know, and it is ultimately up to the police to bring justice to the dead youngster. ‘Broadchurch’ is a fantastic crime drama that will hook you from the first episode. This is the place to go if you’re seeking for a binge-worthy TV in this genre.

    Dexter (2006)


    The protagonist of the show ‘Dexter,’ Dexter Morgan, is a gigantic hypocrite. He works as a forensic technician for the police department during the day and spends the rest of his time committing crimes to feed his homicidal psychopathic tendencies. This show features a lot of comedy and suspense, as well as a lot of drama, action, and suspense. This is a thriller that will keep you on the edge of your seat. ‘Dexter’ is a TV program that should be at the top of your list to watch because of its fantastic storyline and brilliant performers.

    Fargo (2014)


    To all fans of the Coen Brothers’ classic film of the same name, ‘Fargo’ came as a pleasant surprise. With an outstanding cast and plot, this program has a tone and style that is extremely comparable to the original film. But, along with that, it progressively adds something new with each episode, culminating in something even more fascinating and compelling than the film itself. It also makes use of music and dark humor to make subtle references and cues to the original film. The plot twists and turns are practically flawless, and this flawless show can be regarded among the finest.

    Luther (2010)


    The film ‘Luther’ is about DCI John Luther’s friendship with a violent psychopathic killer. The detective has no evidence against the killer, and the two become friends over time, working together to solve crimes. Detective John’s unusual techniques of solving murders constantly get him into problems, resulting in feuds with his superiors. He’s taking a major risk again by befriending a serial murderer, but it might be the only way he can get inside one’s head. The entire show keeps you engrossed throughout, and if you enjoy criminal thrillers, you should not miss it.

    Ray Donovan (2013)


    ‘Ray Donovan’ is the go-to guy for any dirty work that the city’s top players require. Top celebrities, famous athletes, and the world’s wealthiest businesses are among Donovan’s wealthy clients, and they all turn to him when they have troubles. But, as Donovan uses his expertise and skills to solve the problems of the wealthy, he is confronted with his own personal issues when his father is freed from prison. This unanticipated release sets off a chain reaction, resulting in a series of events that will forever alter his life.

    The Bridge (2013)


    A body has been discovered on the border between Denmark and Sweden. Sweden’s Saga Noren and Denmark’s Martin Rhode, both the best in their own nations, must now work together to find the murderer. This is one of the best instances of Nordic Noir available, and it certainly outperforms the majority of mainstream American crime dramas. ‘The Bridge’ is a gripping film throughout, with the one drawback being that it is not in English, so you’ll have to rely on subtitles.

    The Fall (2013)


    Paul, a serial killer who is also a dedicated father, hunts his victims in and around Belfast. This is when Detective Stella enters the picture and is tasked with apprehending the cold-blooded killer. In this scenario, her greatest strength is that, like the killer, she is cold and complex, as well as extremely dedicated to her work. This is a must-read for everyone who like dark criminal thrillers with characters who have real difficulties and struggles. Without a doubt, you will not be disappointed.

    The Killing (2011)


    Detective Sarah Linden is relieved that today is her last day at work and that she and her fiance will be relocating to Sonoma. When a bloody sweater is discovered in the fields and a missing 17-year-old girl called Rosie Larsen is discovered in the trunk of a car at the bottom of the lake, she is obliged to extend her stay. Even worse is the fact that the car is registered in the name of Darren Richmond, a councilman. Sarah agrees to stay in the hopes of finishing this case in a day or two, but she has no idea that this case will be unlike any other she has dealt with before.

    The Wire (2002)


    ‘The Wire,’ set in the city of Baltimore, follows Detective James McNulty and his crew as they investigate different drug-related incidents that plague the city. The show is not just viewed through the eyes of these cops, but also through the eyes of pretty much everyone involved, including politicians, drug lords, law enforcement officers, the media, and ordinary citizens. It would be an exaggeration to call this one the best in the criminal drama genre because it has maintained the quality of its director, photography, and writing throughout its five seasons.

    True Detective (2014)


    Most crime dramas center on the lives of the victims of murders and other crimes, with the adventures of the crime-solving investigators playing a minor role. ‘True Detective,’ on the other hand, takes us on a journey through the personal lives of investigators who have unhealed wounds and bleeding scars from earlier cases. It demonstrates that there is darkness on both sides of the crime, and that crime detectives must often confront their own inner demons and dark secrets. ‘True Detective’ is a fantastic show that you should watch.

    Terriers (2010)


    Hank (Donal Logue) is a former officer who teams up with his best friend Britt (Michael Raymond James) to launch their own private investigative firm. ‘Terriers’ is about the different challenges that these two men face and how they try to avoid taking any responsibility while working hard to run this unauthorized business successfully. Their cunning and humorous relationship will keep you fascinated, and the humour that improves with each episode will undoubtedly make you fall in love with the show.
